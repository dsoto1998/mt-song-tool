⏺ This is a meaty refactor — let me reflect back what I'm hearing and flag the key      
  design questions.                                       
                                                                                        
  ---
  The core problem: You need to play multiple stems simultaneously against a            
  tempo-locked reference (metronome click) to hear if anything is offset. Right now     
  AVPlayer plays one stem at a time with no shared clock and no click, so drift is
  undetectable by ear in the tool.                                                      
                                                          
  ---                                
  AVAudioEngine approach:
  All stems would be AVAudioPlayerNode instances attached to a single AVAudioEngine.    
  Because they share one audio clock, you can schedule all of them with
  scheduleBuffer(_:at:) using a common AVAudioTime — they start sample-accurately. The  
  metronome would be an additional node generating click audio from the Ableton tempo
  map (which you already have from the parser).                                         
                                                          
  ---
